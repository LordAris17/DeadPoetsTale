The Last Photo
The sky rained blood to sweep the smoke of flesh Tears came from the mountains of corpse.
No cry,just the crackling of wood so fresh, As ashes fell where the cities once stood, a silent mesh.
Kamikaze soared above, the heavens split apart, Nuclear drops forged the flame's ruthless art.

The pilot's hand,trembling,reached for the photo, A glimpse of her smile,his heart a shadow.
In the pocket of his uniform,it was there-
A token of love,a memory laid bare, A drop of tears ran down his cheeks, As the winds of fate began to speak.
Through the cockpit's glass,the red smoke bled, A sea of fire where many had bled.
He whispered her name,just once,in the air, Then Dove,a sacrifice beyond compare.
The plane plunged into the oceans's rage, into the red-the final stage.
